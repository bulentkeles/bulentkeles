Project Overview

Our project focuses on delivering a digital solution for Public Bikes, a San Francisco based bicycle retailer. Our goal for this project was to deliver a digital solution that would help Public Bikes sell more of its new line of electric bikes.

The research and designs below explain the whole process we went through during this project. This project was a General Assembly UX Design Immersive assignment and never published on the web.

Problem

Public Bikes is a San Francisco based bike retailer that sells a new line of electric bikes. They believe in their product, however they are having difficulty selling them in the US market. Public is looking to understand what they can do on their website to turn this around.

Approach

Research clearly showed us we needed to educate customers, and allow them to test the e-bike before even considering a purchase because there was very little knowledge about them.

Solution

We created a product comparison module for new customers unaware of e-bikes. We also built a landing page explaining the benefits of an e-bike. And designed a page with curated test rides where customers could easily pick a route to test the e-bike they could easily relate to.

Design Decisions

Our initial reaction to solving the problem was to build a mobile app but that thought quickly faded away as soon as we started studying the user. Research showed users didn't want an app, they needed to know about the e-bike and try it out first. So we build a website that would help site visitors understand the functionalities and benefits of the e-bike instead of just putting price tags on products. We also created a landing page for customers to learn more from other users' experiences and book a test ride so they could experience the benefits first hand.

We also designed a mobile responsive web site so that users could easily book a test ride even on the go. The mobile site is mainly focused on converting users into test riding an e-bike while also giving clear access to product categories.
